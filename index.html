Every training app assumes Monday to Friday. You work a rotating roster, sleep when you can, and your "week" changes every fortnight. Your training plan should keep up — not fall apart.

Features

Your roster is the input.
Your plan is the output.

Enter your shifts once — or sync your roster — and Strongshift does the periodisation for you.

Roster-first planning

Days, nights, lates, 4-on-4-off, FIFO swings. Your plan is built around the shifts you actually work, not an idealised week.

Auto-reshuffle

Picked up an extra shift? Roster changed mid-week? The plan rebuilds itself in seconds — key sessions land on your best days.

Sleep-aware intensity

Night blocks get recovery work, not max-effort intervals. The engine knows a post-nights body isn't ready for PRs.

Strength + running hybrid

Progressive lifting and structured running in one plan — programmed together so they don't cannibalise each other.

Shift-length sessions

25-minute pre-night primers. 35-minute post-shift lifts. Full sessions saved for days off. Every workout fits the day it's on.

Progress that survives nights

Track lifts, run times and consistency across roster cycles — see progress by cycle, not by an arbitrary calendar week.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

When does Strongshift launch?

Q4 2026. Waitlist members get first access to the beta, plus the founding member discount at launch.

Which roster patterns does it handle?

All of them — straight days, permanent nights, rotating rosters, 4-on-4-off, FIFO swings, on-call blocks. If you can enter it, the engine can program around it.

What happens when my roster changes mid-week?

Update your shifts and the week rebuilds instantly. Key sessions move to your best recovery windows; nothing heavy lands after a run of nights.

Do I need a gym?

No. Choose a gym, home-equipment or bodyweight track when you set up. Hospital gym at 3am counts too.

Who is building this?

A critical care doctor in Brisbane who has spent years training for strength and running goals around a hospital roster — and got tired of apps that assume weekends exist.

What does it cost?
